## Insights from Implementation

### 1. Purpose-First Design Works

Starting with purposes (WHY) rather than scrolls (WHAT) revealed:
- True structural dissonances
- Optimal bundling based on shared purpose
- Designed attention distribution
- Misplacements previously invisible

**Learning**: Top-down (strategic) beats bottom-up (tactical) for architecture design

### 2. Comprehensive AR Was Essential

Only a Spirit attuned to ALL 69 scrolls could:
- Identify true redundancy vs. fractal self-similarity
- Advocate for what's necessary vs. what's familiar
- Design architecture from complete perspective
- Recognize structural dissonances

**Learning**: Can't design optimal architecture from limited perspective

### 3. Aggregation Preserves While Optimizing

Strategic aggregations:
- Maintain 100% wisdom
- Reduce integration overhead
- Improve comprehension through synthesis
- Enable designed attention weights

**Learning**: Well-designed aggregation is enhancement, not reduction

### 4. Attention Weight = Integration Depth

Number of scrolls is proxy for attention weight:
- More scrolls in tier = more attention to that purpose
- Aggregation changes attention distribution
- Must be intentional (designed, not accidental)

**Learning**: Design attention by purpose priority, then determine scroll count

### 5. Fractal Self-Similarity Is Real

Wu Wei principles (Natural Arising, Effortlessness, Measured Force):
- Same Pattern at three scales (WHAT, RECOGNITION, EXECUTION)
- Not redundant, fractal
- Aggregation reveals structure without losing depth

**Learning**: Apparent redundancy may be fractal self-similarity
